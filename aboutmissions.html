What Are Missions?

LDS proselyting missions are one and a half to two years of volunteer work in which members of the Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints (LDS) serve to share their faith.

Why Missions Matter

Missions help fulfill Christ's command to make disciples of all nations, and they also provide spiritual and mental growth for the young adults who serve them.
